Welcome to Chhattisgarh

Chhattisgarh, a state located in central India, is increasingly becoming a sought-after destination for tourists, thanks to its rich cultural heritage, diverse landscapes, and serene environment. Known for its unspoiled natural beauty, Chhattisgarh offers a unique blend of wildlife, waterfalls, temples, and tribal culture that attracts travelers looking for both adventure and tranquility.

Chitrakote Waterfall

Known as the 'Niagara of India,' Chitrakote Waterfall is the widest waterfall in India. Located on the Indravati River, it offers breathtaking views, especially during the monsoon season.

The surrounding forests and tribal villages add to the charm of this natural wonder.

Bastar Tribal Culture

Bastar is famous for its unique tribal culture, arts, and festivals. The Dussehra celebration in Bastar is one of the most distinctive and longest festivals in the region.

The local handicrafts, including bell metal, terracotta, and wood carvings, are globally acclaimed.

Kanger Valley National Park

Kanger Valley National Park is a paradise for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts. It is home to dense forests, stunning caves, and diverse flora and fauna.

The park also houses the famous Kutumsar and Kailash caves, adorned with mesmerizing stalactites and stalagmites.

Sirpur Heritage Site

Sirpur, an ancient town, is renowned for its archaeological significance and beautifully crafted temples. The Laxman Temple, built in the 7th century, stands as an architectural marvel.

Sirpur's ruins reflect the state's rich history and its importance as a center of Buddhism and Hinduism.
