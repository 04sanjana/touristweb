Welcome to Assam

Assam, a northeastern state of India, is a treasure trove of natural beauty, wildlife, and cultural heritage. It is home to the world-famous **Kaziranga National Park**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its one-horned rhinoceroses and diverse wildlife. The **Majuli River Island**, one of the largest river islands in the world, offers stunning landscapes and rich cultural traditions. The state's tea gardens in places like **Jorhat** are a delight for nature lovers, while the mighty **Brahmaputra River** provides scenic beauty and river cruises. **Kamakhya Temple** in Guwahati is a revered spiritual site attracting pilgrims and tourists alike. With its mix of wildlife, historical landmarks, and vibrant culture, Assam is a must-visit destination for travelers.

Kaziranga National Park

Kaziranga National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the most renowned wildlife sanctuaries in India. Located on the banks of the Brahmaputra River, it is home to the world's largest population of one-horned rhinoceroses. The park is also known for its diverse flora and fauna, including tigers, elephants, and various species of birds.

Visitors can enjoy safaris in open jeeps or on elephant back, providing a close-up view of the park's wildlife and its stunning landscape.

Tea Gardens of Assam

Assam is renowned for its tea plantations, which are among the largest in the world. The state's lush tea gardens, set against the backdrop of rolling hills, offer a serene environment for visitors to explore. Assam tea is known for its strong flavor and distinct aroma.

The tea gardens provide an opportunity for visitors to experience the rural life of Assam, while also enjoying the picturesque surroundings.

Majuli Island

Majuli is the largest river island in the world, located on the Brahmaputra River. It is known for its unique cultural heritage, vibrant satras (Vaishnavite monasteries), and scenic beauty. Majuli is also famous for its diverse birdlife, making it a haven for birdwatchers.

Visitors can explore the island's lush green landscapes, interact with local communities, and enjoy cultural performances and festivals that highlight the rich traditions of Assam.

Kamakhya Temple

The Kamakhya Temple, located on Nilachal Hill in Guwahati, is one of the most important pilgrimage sites in Assam and India. It is dedicated to the goddess Kamakhya, one of the oldest and most revered deities in the Hindu religion. The temple is famous for its ancient architecture and spiritual significance, attracting thousands of pilgrims each year.

The temple complex offers a peaceful atmosphere with stunning views of the surrounding hills and Brahmaputra River. It is especially crowded during the Ambubachi Mela, an annual festival that draws devotees from all over the country.
