Welcome to Bihar

Bihar, a state rich in history and culture, is home to several prominent tourist destinations that attract visitors from around the world. Bodh Gaya, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is one of the most significant places, where Lord Buddha attained enlightenment under the Bodhi Tree. Nalanda, known for the ancient Nalanda University, reflects the glorious academic heritage of India. Rajgir, surrounded by lush hills, is a spiritual hub for Buddhists, Jains, and Hindus. The Mahabodhi Temple, Vikramshila Ruins, and the ancient city of Vaishali are also popular attractions. Bihar's historical landmarks, spiritual centers, and cultural richness make it a must-visit for travelers.

Mahabodhi Temple

The Mahabodhi Temple in Bodh Gaya is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a sacred place of pilgrimage for Buddhists. It marks the location where Lord Buddha attained enlightenment under the Bodhi Tree.

The temple complex features stunning architecture and serene surroundings that attract visitors from all over the world.

Nalanda University

Nalanda University, one of the oldest centers of learning in the world, dates back to the 5th century. It was a renowned seat of education and attracted scholars from across Asia.

Today, the ruins of Nalanda stand as a testament to the glorious history of Bihar and its role in spreading knowledge and culture.

Rajgir

Rajgir is a historical and spiritual destination surrounded by hills and natural beauty. It was an important site in the lives of Lord Buddha and Lord Mahavira.

Visitors can explore ancient monuments, hot springs, and the tranquil Vulture's Peak, making Rajgir a must-visit destination in Bihar.

Vikramshila University

Vikramshila University was one of the most prominent centers of Buddhist learning during the Pala Empire. It played a key role in education and cultural exchange during ancient times.

The ruins of the university showcase its architectural grandeur and significance in history.
