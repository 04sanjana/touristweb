Welcome to Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh stands as India's most remote state, where the first sunrays embrace Indian soil each morning. Tucked away in northeast India's Arunachal Pradesh is home to breathtaking landscapes, unique cultures, and unexplored horizons - China, Bhutan and Myanmar share borders which create stunning biodiversity scenes along its borders with Arunachal Pradesh as a result. What sets Arunachal Pradesh apart as India's hidden gem lies within how well-preserved ancient customs remain; mist-kissed hills, meandering rivers and cascading waterfalls all create magical moments across this extraordinary terrain!.

Tawang Monastery

The Tawang Monastery, located in the Tawang district, is the largest monastery in India and one of the largest in the world. It is a significant center of Tibetan Buddhism and is home to several monks and pilgrims. The monastery is surrounded by snow-capped mountains, offering a tranquil and spiritual atmosphere.

The monastery's architecture is impressive, featuring intricate Tibetan designs. Visitors can also witness the sacred Tawang War Memorial nearby, which commemorates the sacrifices made by the Indian army in the 1962 India-China war.

Bumla Pass

Bumla Pass is a high-altitude mountain pass located on the Indo-China border, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding snow-capped peaks. It holds historical significance as it was a key military route during the 1962 Sino-Indian war. Visitors can experience the beauty of nature, the serenity of the surroundings, and the proximity to the border.

This is an ideal place for adventure enthusiasts and nature lovers, as it allows you to explore the Himalayas, enjoy the crisp mountain air, and witness the rugged terrains of Arunachal Pradesh.

Ziro Valley

Ziro Valley, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is known for its picturesque rice fields, scenic views, and the vibrant Apatani tribal culture. It is located in the Lower Subansiri district and is a popular destination for nature lovers, photographers, and those looking to experience rural life in Arunachal Pradesh.

The valley is famous for its unique tribal customs, sustainable agricultural practices, and the annual Ziro Festival of Music, which attracts music lovers from around the world. Visitors can explore the valley's lush green landscapes, ancient temples, and local markets.

Sangti Valley

Sangti Valley is one of Arunachal Pradesh's hidden gems, located in the Tawang district. It is a remote, peaceful valley known for its lush green fields, crystal-clear rivers, and the perfect backdrop of snow-capped mountains. The valley is home to a small but vibrant tribal community and offers opportunities for eco-tourism, trekking, and birdwatching.

Sangti Valley provides an off-the-beaten-path experience, with its serene environment making it a great place for meditation and relaxation. It's a perfect spot for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and immerse themselves in nature.
